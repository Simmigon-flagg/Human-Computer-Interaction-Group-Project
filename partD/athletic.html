Sports News

Basketball

JSWT University will face off against Georgia State University on May 6, 2016. Last year, the Panthers took home the win with a final score of 76-74. Head coach Matt Smith says the team has been training hard for this game. "This is one of the biggest games of the season. A lot of people come out to watch us play and we want to give it our all," Smith said. Come out next Saturday to support JSWT University athletics.

Football

The first round of the NFL Draft is April 30 and for the first time in 10 years, JSWT University will have a player taken in the opening round. Jack Smith has proven to be the driving force behind JSWT University's football team. Tune in to ESPN April 30 at 8 p.m. to watch NFL Commissioner Roger Goodell make the announcements.

Gymnastics

JSWT University senior Charlotte Johnson is among the final four candidates for gymnastics' Honda Award. The Honda Award is given to the top collegiate female athlete in 12 different sports each season. The Honda Sports Award is presented annually by the CWSA to the top women athletes in 12 NCAA- sanctioned sports and signifies "the best of the best in collegiate athletics". The winner of the sport award becomes a finalist for the Collegiate Woman Athlete of the Year and the prestigious 2016 Honda Cup. The announcement of the winner will be sent out via email Monday, May 2.
